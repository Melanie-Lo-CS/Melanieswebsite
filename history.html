History of BTS

This group is popular for having members who are talented, with ability to compose, write and perform their own composition or music. All the members have participated in one way or another in making Bangtanâ€™s albums become a huge success. Those members with active roles are Rap monster, SUGA and J-HOPE who participate in composing and writing a number of their releases. BTS is well rounded because each member has his own personality that helps to bring the group to life. They have photographers, rap composers, artists and chefs in their midst. The team is known to be multilingual; Rap Monster speaks Japanese and also fluent in English, V speaks Japanese, both V and J-HOPE are learning Mandarin, and Jin speaks Mandarin. Before joining BTS, J-HOPE was involved in underground dancing battles and competitions. SUGA and Rap Monster were involved in underground rapping battles and competitions. Most of the members were active on their respective interests including rapping, vocalist, dancing, acting and singing. When they went for auditions, which begun in 2010 to 2011, the members showcased their talent and won the hearts of the judges. Although the group has gone through a number of changes since the first audition, the final group was formed in 2012. The group has built its name and fan base through video blogs, fancafe posts, twitter, and uploading their new songs on SoundCloud and YouTube. BTS released their first album on June 12th 2013 called 2COOL4SKOOL. The group made a comeback on September 11th 2013 with a 10 track album known as O!RUL8 2?. On November 6th 2013 they released The Rise Of Bangtan. BTS starred in their own show called Rookie King that premiered on 3rd September, which had 8 episodes.
